About Us

“A Place Older Than Time.” Nestled deep within the heart of untouched wilderness, Elder Glen National Park has been a protected site for generations. Known for its towering ancient forests, mist-covered trails, and rich biodiversity, the park offers a truly immersive natural experience unlike any other.

Our Mission At Elder Glen: we are committed to preserving the land, protecting its history, and ensuring a safe yet unforgettable experience for every visitor. Our dedicated rangers and conservationists work tirelessly to maintain the park’s delicate ecosystem while allowing the public to witness the beauty of this truly unique landscape.

A History Shrouded in Mystery: Elder Glen was officially designated as a national park in the early 1900s, though its history stretches back much further. Indigenous folklore speaks of the land as a place of deep reverence, where the earth itself watches. Early settlers recorded strange occurrences—unusual sounds in the trees, paths that seemed to shift, and travelers who would take an afternoon hike only to return days later, unable to recall where they had been.

What Makes Elder Glen Unique?

  • Flora & Fauna Found Nowhere Else – Many species in the park cannot be found in any other region. Scientists continue to study the unique environmental conditions that make this possible.
  • Ancient Landmarks – Certain trees within the park have been estimated to be thousands of years old, with some featuring unexplained carvings of unknown origin.
  • A Timeless Experience – Visitors often remark on the unusual stillness of the Glen, the way time seems to slow, and the indescribable feeling of being watched—but never unwelcome.

Plan Your Visit: Whether you’re here for a peaceful retreat into nature, an adventurous hike through the misty hills, or simply to experience a place with a history as deep as the roots beneath your feet, Elder Glen welcomes you. One final note – We ask all visitors to respect the land and its mysteries. If you find a trail that does not appear on the map, do not follow it. If you hear whispers in the trees, it is just the wind. And if you feel like you are being watched…Know that you are not alone.

Meet Our Team!

“Dedicated to Preservation, Committed to Safety.”

The staff at Elder Glen National Park work tirelessly to maintain the park’s beauty, guide visitors, and ensure everyone’s safety during their stay. Our rangers and conservationists are highly trained professionals who know the land better than anyone—and they take their duty seriously.

Featured Staff Members

Chief Ranger Elias Moore

With over 25 years of experience, Ranger Moore has dedicated his life to preserving Elder Glen. When asked about his most memorable experience in the park, he simply smiled and said, “There are some things best left undiscovered.”

Lead Ecologist Dr. Miriam Halloway

Specializing in rare plant species, Dr. Halloway has documented flora found nowhere else on Earth within the Glen. When asked about certain species that seem to “move” or “appear where they shouldn’t,” she responded, “Nature is full of surprises.”

Night Ranger Daniel Reese

Tasked with overseeing the park after dark, Ranger Reese leads our popular night tours and monitors the deeper parts of the Glen. He reminds visitors that if they hear something rustling off the trail, it’s most likely an animal.

Firewatch Officer Claire Sutton

Stationed at the Veilwood Lookout, Officer Sutton spends weeks at a time alone in the tower, keeping watch for wildfires. Visitors often ask if it’s lonely up there, to which she replies, “I’m never alone.”
