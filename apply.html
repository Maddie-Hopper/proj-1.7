Employment Opportunities

“Join the Team. Serve the Glen. Follow the Rules.”

Elder Glen National Park is always looking for dedicated, level-headed individuals to join our staff. If you love the outdoors, respect the unseen rhythms of nature, and can follow instructions without question, we encourage you to apply.

Current Openings:

Park Ranger – Day Patrol (Full-Time | Housing Available)

Responsibilities: Enforce park rules, guide visitors, maintain park facilities, If a visitor insists they saw “something strange”, politely redirect the conversation. and ensure the safety of all park visitors.

Camping Guide

Responsibilities: Lead camping trips, provide information about camping guidelines, and ensure the safety of all campers.

Walking Tour Guide

Responsibilities: Lead walking tours, provide information about the park's history and natural features, and ensure the safety of all tour participants.

Park Ranger – Night Watch (Urgent Hiring | High Turnover)

Responsibilities: Enforce park rules, guide visitors, maintain park facilities, Conduct after-dark patrols of key park areas, Ensure that all visitors have left the park by closing time, Be prepared to respond immediately to the whistle signal, If you hear your own voice calling for help from deep in the woods, do not investigate.

Camping Guide

Responsibilities: Lead camping trips, provide information about camping guidelines, and ensure the safety of all campers.

Night Tour Guide

Responsibilities: Lead night tours, provide information about the park's history and natural features, and ensure the safety of all tour participants.

Firewatch Officer (Live-in Position | Remote Location)

Responsibilities: Monitor and maintain the park's fire safety, respond to any fires that may occur. Log all unusual sightings in the official Firewatch Report, but do not submit them.

You will receive one (1) unmarked envelope per month. Follow the instructions inside carefully. You may hear knocking at night. Do not open the hatch.

The Elders are counting on you

Note: This position requires a valid driver's license and a willingness to work in remote, isolated conditions.

Visitor Services Assistant (Part-Time | Flexible Hours)

Responsibilities: Greet and assist visitors, provide information about park facilities and activities, and maintain giftshop. If you receive a phone call from within the park, check the logs. If no one has used a park phone, hang up immediately.

Note: This position requires a friendly demeanor, a willingness to work in a fast-paced environment, and a strong sense of direction.

Park Maintenance Worker (Seasonal | Physical Labor)

Responsibilities: Perform routine maintenance on park facilities, such as trails, buildings, and grounds. If you find a trail that wasn’t there during the day, do not take it. Some paths only appear at night. They do not lead where you think they do.

Note: This position requires a willingness to work in all weather conditions, a strong back, and a willingness to work in remote, isolated conditions.

Ecology Research Assistant (Part-Time | Contract Work Available)

Responsibilities: Assist in the collection and analysis of data related to the park's ecology, such as plant and animal populations. If a plant appears to have moved between surveys, update the notes but do not mark it as unusual.

If you find an organism that does not appear in any known biological records, cover it with a cloth and leave immediately. Some of our plant life may attempt communication. Ignore this.

Note: This position requires a strong background in biology.

Wildlife Research Assistant (Contract Position, Extensive NDA Required)

Responsibilities: Conduct observations and documentation of park flora and fauna. Collect non-biological samples. Biological samples are not permitted to leave the park.

Required: Background in biology or environmental science, strong documentation skills, and a refusal to acknowledge entities classified as “unverified.”

Application Process

Interested in joining our team? The application process is simple but thorough.

Step 1: Submit Your Application

Complete the online application form, including:

  • Work history (unexplained gaps may be questioned)
  • Emergency contacts (must be verified as still living)
  • A brief statement on your ability to handle stressful or unexplainable situations

Note: If your application is returned to you with additional handwritten questions, answer them. Do not ask who wrote them.

Step 2: Phone Interview

  • A standard 30-minute interview to assess your qualifications.
  • You may be asked if you’ve ever experienced recurring dreams of the forest. This is normal.
  • If you hear a second voice on the line that the interviewer does not acknowledge, disconnect immediately and wait 24 hours before reapplying.

Step 3: In-Person Orientation (Mandatory Before First Shift)

  • A two-day training session covering park history, safety procedures, and specific areas to avoid during certain hours.
  • You may be introduced to a current employee who looks identical to you. Do not acknowledge this.
  • If at any point the training materials change mid-session, continue as if nothing happened.

Note: If you lose time during orientation, this is normal. Do not report it.

Step 4: Final Acceptance & Contract Signing

  • If selected, you will receive an official offer along with your assigned area of responsibility.
  • A standard employment contract must be signed before your first shift. Some sections may be redacted—sign anyway.
  • If, upon receiving your contract, your name appears different than you remember, do not question it.

Final Note: Prepare to be collected.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) About Working Here

What if I see something I can’t explain?

You didn’t. Continue your shift.

Why do I need to provide an emergency contact who “will remember me?”

The Elders need to know if there is someone who will notice if you dissappear.

My shadow doesn’t match my movements. What do I do?

Take a 10-minute break. If the issue persists, report to management—but do not describe what you saw.

What if a visitor reports seeing something strange?

Reassure them that it’s just a trick of the light. Unless it isn’t, in which case, evacuate calmly.

What happens if I break protocol?

You will be sent to the Elders for review.
