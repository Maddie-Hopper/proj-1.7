Camping Guidelines

Camping at Elder Glen National Park

“Stay the Night. If You Dare.”

For the brave and adventurous, overnight camping is permitted in designated areas of Elder Glen National Park. Experience the unparalleled beauty of the park under starlit skies, but remember—the rules are there for a reason.

Spending the night in Elder Glen National Park is a truly unforgettable experience. Beneath a sky untouched by city lights, the park offers serene beauty, crisp night air, and an ambiance unlike any other. However, the forest does not sleep when you do. Before setting up camp, make sure you understand the rules, recommendations, and warnings

CAMPING RULES AND GUIDELINES

  • Stick to the designated campsites. – If you find an “unlisted” campsite with a perfectly made fire pit and a half-built tent, do not stay there.
  • Follow "Leave No Trace" principles. – Pick up all trash, including food scraps. Do not disturb or remove natural objects.
  • Respect wildlife. – Do not feed or approach animals. Store food and trash securely.
  • Be prepared for the elements. – Bring appropriate clothing, rain gear, and a first aid kit.
  • Do not camp alone. – Even if you’re an experienced camper, the Elders prefer groups
  • Check your tent before entering. – Sometimes, there are things inside that weren’t there when you left.
  • If you hear someone whisper your name from outside your tent, do not respond. – No one should be wandering at night.
  • Do not accept gifts from the forest. – If you wake up to find an object sitting outside your tent, leave it there.
  • If you find a trail that wasn’t there during the day, do not take it. – Some paths only appear at night. They do not lead where you think they do.
  • Stay by the fire. – Darkness is not always empty.

CAMPGROUND OPTIONS

Elder Glen offers three official camping areas. Choose wisely—each has its own history.

Veilwood Campground (Family-Friendly, Closest to Visitor Center)

  • Located just a short hike from the Visitor Center, this well-maintained campground is best for beginners.
  • Features fire pits, picnic tables, and maintained trails.
  • Some campers report hearing soft singing from the trees at night. This is normal and nothing to worry about.
  • If you wake up to find a small stone circle near your tent, leave it undisturbed.

  • Best For: Families, casual campers, those who don’t want to be too far from safety.

Hollow Glen Campsites (Remote, True Wilderness Experience)

  • A secluded series of primitive campsites located deep in the woods.
  • No electricity, no running water—just you and nature.
  • The stars shine brighter here, but sometimes, they appear in the wrong places.
  • Unmarked trails may appear between the trees at night. If you see one, do not take it.

  • Best For: Experienced campers, those seeking total isolation, those who enjoy being observed.

Silent Divines Campground (Restricted – Special Permit Required)

  • Located in a clearing that does not always appear on maps.
  • No fires allowed—this is for your safety.
  • Rangers check on campers at sunrise. If you are not where you were the night before, stay where you are. We will find you.

Ranger led Workshops

Ranger-Led Survival Skills Workshops

“Learn the Land. Adapt to the Unknown.”

For those seeking a deeper connection to the wilderness, Elder Glen National Park offers hands-on survival workshops led by our experienced rangers. These sessions will teach you essential outdoor skills while ensuring you can handle unexpected situations—because out here, you never know what you might encounter.

Workshop Options Overview

  • Fire-Building 101
  • Navigation Basics
  • Emergency Shelter Construction
  • Wildlife Tracking
  • First Aid for the Outdoors
  • Survival Cooking
  • Emergency Signal Techniques
  • Leave No Trace Principles
